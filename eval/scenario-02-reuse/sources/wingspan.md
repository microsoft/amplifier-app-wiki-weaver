# Wingspan

Wingspan is a competitive card-driven engine-building board game designed by Elizabeth Hargrave
and published by Stonemaier Games in 2019. It won the Kennerspiel des Jahres award in 2019,
bringing engine-building mechanics to a wider audience through its accessible bird theme.

## Overview

Players are bird enthusiasts — researchers, bird watchers, and collectors — seeking to discover
and attract the best birds to their wildlife preserves. The game supports 1–5 players and
typically takes **40–70 minutes** per game for experienced players.

## Habitats and Engine Building

Birds are played to one of three habitat rows: forest (gaining food), grassland (laying eggs),
and wetland (drawing cards). Each bird has a power that triggers when the corresponding habitat
action is taken. Over four rounds, these chained powers form increasingly efficient engines.

## Card Diversity

The game features over 170 unique bird cards based on real North American species, each with
accurate wingspan measurements, diet, and nesting type. This biological accuracy contributes
to the game's crossover appeal beyond traditional strategy game audiences.

## Why Wingspan Stands Out

Wingspan demonstrated that engine building could be approachable without sacrificing depth.
Its bird theme provides genuine cognitive scaffolding — card powers echo each species' real
ecological role — reducing the abstraction barrier that often accompanies engine games.
