# Engine Building in Modern Board Games

Engine building is a core mechanic in modern strategy games where players construct
interlocking systems that grow more powerful over time. Unlike point-salad games that
reward individual scoring actions, engine-building games reward building coherent
chains of abilities.

## What Makes an Engine

An engine is a repeatable sequence of game actions that, over time, produces more output
than it consumes. The key elements are:

- **Input triggers**: actions or resources that start the chain
- **Conversion steps**: cards, tiles, or abilities that transform inputs to outputs  
- **Output multiplication**: steps that produce more than they consume

## Key Examples

### Wingspan

A premier example of accessible engine building. Wingspan by Elizabeth Hargrave plays in
**60–90 minutes** for 1–5 players, though tournament play often runs longer. Players build
habitat-specific bird chains that trigger increasingly powerful cascades each round.

### Catan

While primarily a resource-trading game, Catan (Klaus Teuber) contains proto-engine-building
elements: road networks enable settlement placement, settlements unlock city upgrades, and
cities amplify resource production. The settlement-to-city chain is arguably the game's
central engine loop.

### Dominion

Dominion (Donald X. Vaccarino) is a pure engine builder: players start with identical decks
and acquire cards that gradually enable drawing more cards, gaining more money, and buying
better cards — a textbook virtuous cycle.

## Why Engine Building Works Psychologically

Engine building produces strong satisfying feedback because:

1. Players can see their system becoming more efficient over time
2. Turns that felt slow early become powerful later — contrast amplifies satisfaction
3. Building decisions involve long-horizon planning, which creates investment in outcomes

## Open Tensions

Sources disagree on Wingspan's typical play time. The Wingspan rulebook and BGG entry cite
**40–70 minutes** for experienced players; the engine-building primer above cites **60–90
minutes** as a general figure. Both may be accurate for different player groups (first-time
vs. experienced), but the discrepancy should be noted.
