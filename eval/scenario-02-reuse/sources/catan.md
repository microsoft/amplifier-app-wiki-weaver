# Catan

Catan (formerly The Settlers of Catan) is a multiplayer board game designed by Klaus Teuber,
first published in 1995. It is widely credited with bringing the German board game movement
to mass-market audiences worldwide.

## Overview

Players take on the roles of settlers establishing colonies on the island of Catan. The game
supports 3–4 players (6 with an expansion) and typically takes 60–120 minutes per session.

## Resource Economy

Players collect five types of resource cards — brick, lumber, ore, grain, and wool — obtained
by dice rolls that activate hexagonal terrain tiles. Trading with other players and the bank
is central to the game's social dynamic.

## Building and Victory Points

Settlements (1 VP), cities (2 VP), roads, and special cards (Longest Road, Largest Army) are
the paths to the game's 10-point victory target. The branching build order creates meaningful
strategic decisions each turn.

## Why Catan Matters

Catan proved that complex strategy games could reach mainstream audiences. Its approachable
ruleset, strong social interaction, and variable map setup made it a gateway game for millions
of players entering the hobby.
